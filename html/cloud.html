The x axis displays the range of putative minimum free energy values computed for all [% longspecies %] sequences of length [% seqlength %].
The y axis displays the range of z scores for these same sequences.
Each dot thus represents a single (mfe, z score) in relation to all the others for [% longspecies %]. The black lines delineate the mean mfe and z score for this population. [% IF slipsites %] The black dots represent all sequence windows with a [% slipsites %] slippery site. [% ELSE %] This graph shows all points regardless of slipsite. [% END %] Grey dots represent sequence windows which have less putative secondary structure than the mean and are less structured than their randomized counterparts, as such these are unlikely to be of interest. Green dots have a minimum free energy less than the mean, but are less structured than their randomized counterparts while the opposite is true of blue dots. Red dots are more significant than the mean in both respects. Grey lines delineate standard deviations less than mean. Darker dots represent coordinates wihch share more than 1 sequence window.
